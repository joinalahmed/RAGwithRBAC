### 2. Cost Structure Analysis

**Fixed Costs:**
- **Salaries and Wages:** $600,000 annually
- **Rent and Utilities:** $120,000 annually
- **Insurance:** $30,000 annually
- **Depreciation:** $50,000 annually

**Variable Costs:**
- **Cost of Goods Sold (COGS):** 40% of revenue
- **Marketing Expenses:** 15% of revenue
- **Sales Commissions:** 5% of revenue

**Cost Structure Breakdown:**
- Year 1 Total Costs = Fixed Costs + Variable Costs
    - COGS: $600,000 (40% of $1,500,000)
    - Marketing: $225,000 (15% of $1,500,000)
    - Sales Commissions: $75,000 (5% of $1,500,000)
  
Total Costs Year 1 = $600,000 + $120,000 + $30,000 + $50,000 + $600,000 + $225,000 + $75,000 = $1,700,000

### 4. Risk Assessments

**Key Risks:**
- **Market Risk:** Changes in consumer preferences and competitive landscape can affect revenue.
- **Operational Risk:** Supply chain disruptions could increase COGS and impact profitability.
- **Financial Risk:** Fluctuations in interest rates could affect financing costs for long-term investments.

**Mitigation Strategies:**
- Diversify product offerings and markets to reduce dependency on any single revenue stream.
- Build strong relationships with multiple suppliers to mitigate supply chain risks.
- Maintain a liquidity buffer equivalent to six months of operating expenses to handle financial uncertainties.
